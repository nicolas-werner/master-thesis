Instructions to the Legislator.

It is not clear that in every one of the several titles of
Law above mentioned there will need a provision or set of
provisions authorising one person to bring up on the pers-
on of another. To determine that question will require to
say of them would be to enter upon a branch of law for-
eign to that in hand. But with respect to every one of
these titles could such provisions be included in themselves
in any book of universal Law: since they will need to be
different according to the various local circumstances of dif-
ferent states and provinces. The above references are col-
lected only in the way of example, to give a general idea
of the mode of reference to be adopted in on on see-
sion, by the present.
